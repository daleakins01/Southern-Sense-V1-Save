Scent Glossary

Ever wonder what "notes" mean in a fragrance? Or what the difference is between a "woody" and an "earthy" scent? Our scent glossary is here to guide you.

The Scent Families

Understanding Scent Notes

A fragrance is a blend of different ingredients, called "notes." These notes unfold over time in three stages, creating the full experience of the scent.

  • Top Notes: This is your first impression. They are the lightest notes and fade the fastest. Common top notes include citrus (lemon, bergamot) and light herbs (mint, basil).
  • Middle Notes (or "Heart Notes"): These notes emerge as the top notes fade. They form the "heart" of the fragrance and are usually fuller and more rounded. Common middle notes include florals (rose, jasmine) and spices (cinnamon, clove).
  • Base Notes: These are the deep, rich notes that anchor the scent and last the longest. They provide the final, lasting impression. Common base notes include woods (sandalwood, cedar), vanilla, musk, and amber.

Other Terms to Know

Scent Throw
This refers to how well a candle's fragrance fills a room. A "strong throw" will fill a large space, while a "subtle throw" is better for a small room.
Wax Melt
A wickless piece of scented wax that is gently heated in a wax warmer to release its fragrance. A great flame-free alternative to candles.
