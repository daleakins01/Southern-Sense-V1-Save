Candle Care 101

Get the Best Burn

For Your Candles

The First Burn is Key

Allow the wax to melt all the way to the edge of the container on the very first burn. This creates a "melt pool" and prevents "tunneling," ensuring an even burn and the best scent throw for the life of the candle.

Trim Your Wick

Before each new burn, trim the wooden wick to about 1/4 inch. This prevents a high flame, reduces soot, and gives you a cleaner, brighter burn. Gently snap off the charred tip with your fingers or wick trimmers.

Burn Safely

Never leave a burning candle unattended. Keep it on a stable, heat-resistant surface away from drafts, children, and pets. Do not burn for more than 4 hours at a time.

For Your Wax Melts

Use the Right Amount

Start with one or two cubes from your wax melt clamshell. Using too much can cause the warmer dish to overflow. Our melts are highly scented to provide a rich aroma that fills the room without being overwhelming.

Changing Your Scent

When the fragrance has faded, it's time to change the wax. The easiest way is to let the wax cool and harden completely, then gently warm the dish for a minute. The solid wax puck should slide right out. Alternatively, while the wax is melted, place a few cotton balls in the warmer to soak it up, then wipe clean.
