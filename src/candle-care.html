Candle Care 101

To get the most out of your Southern Sense candle, follow these simple steps. Proper care ensures a clean, long-lasting burn and the best possible fragrance experience.

1. The First Burn is Key

When you light your candle for the first time, allow it to burn until the wax pool melts all the way to the edge of the container. This can take 2-4 hours. This prevents "tunneling" (when the candle burns straight down the middle) and ensures an even burn for the life of the candle.

2. Trim Your Wick

Before every single burn, trim the wick to about 1/4 inch. This prevents excessive smoking, sooting (the black stuff), and a dangerously large flame. You can use a wick trimmer, nail clippers, or even your fingers to pinch off the charred tip.

3. Watch Your Burn Time

Don't burn your candle for more than 4 hours at a time. After 4 hours, extinguish the flame, let the candle cool completely, trim the wick, and then you're ready to re-light.

4. Burn Safely

Always burn your candle on a stable, heat-resistant surface away from drafts, pets, children, and flammable materials. Never leave a burning candle unattended.

5. Know When to Say Goodbye

Discontinue use when only 1/2 inch of wax remains at the bottom of the jar. Burning it lower than this is a fire hazard. It's time for a new candle! (And don't forget, you can clean out the jar and participate in our Refill Program.)
