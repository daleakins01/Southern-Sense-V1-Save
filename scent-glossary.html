Scent Glossary

The Art of Fragrance

The Scent Pyramid

Fragrances are built like a pyramid, with three levels of "notes" that unfold over time. This structure creates a complete, evolving scent experience.

Top Notes (The Introduction)

These are the first notes you smell. They are light, fresh, and evaporate quickly, forming the initial impression. (e.g., Lemon, Bergamot, Mint)

Middle Notes (The Heart)

The "heart" of the fragrance. These notes appear as the top notes fade, are usually well-rounded, and make up the main body of the scent. (e.g., Lavender, Jasmine, Rose)

Base Notes (The Foundation)

These are the deepest, richest notes. They are slow to evaporate, provide depth, and are the final scent you'll notice. (e.g., Vanilla, Sandalwood, Musk, Cedar)

Common Notes We Love

Bergamot

A bright, zesty citrus note from the Bergamot orange. It's uplifting, slightly spicy, and adds a sparkling, fresh quality.

Cedarwood

A classic woody note. It's dry, warm, and has a "pencil-shavings" aroma that provides a solid, grounding base.

Coconut

Creamy, sweet, and tropical. This note instantly adds a lactonic, rich, and beachy feel to a fragrance.

Musk

A very common base note. It doesn't have a strong scent on its own, but adds a clean, "your-skin-but-better" warmth and stability to a fragrance.

Jasmine

A classic, rich, and intensely floral note. It's sweet, romantic, and slightly musky, adding a touch of timeless elegance.

Sandalwood

A creamy, soft, and warm woody note. It's smoother and sweeter than cedarwood, providing a comforting, luxurious, and sensual base.

Vanilla

A familiar, sweet, and comforting scent. Beyond just "bakery," a good vanilla can be creamy, smoky, or even floral, adding warmth and depth to any blend.
